chicken

Chicken fried rice is the comfort dish of Chinese food. It's been around for a very long time and is also common in East, Southeast, and South Asian cuisines.
The dish started as way to use leftover fried rice that has dried out some and may not be great on its own, but is still perfectly edible. The addition of oil and soy sauce and veggies and meat spruce up the rice for a delicious meal.
A traditional Chinese fried rice would be made in a wok over high heat, but we've made ours in a cast iron skillet since they are more common in home kitchens. This take on the favorite is easy to make and makes the perfect lunch or dinner. Don't want chicken? Make it with shrimp or go vegetarian and make it with pineapple!

Growing up, I hated nuts. My mom tried to feed me all kinds of nuts because they were supposedly good for me: walnuts for brain health, almonds for skin health. I was 6 years old and invincible and did not need any of these health boosters that tasted like solidified oils! As I grew older, I finally understood my mom's confusion at my disdain for nuts. Now I cannot live a single day without snacking on some. Moral of the story: Mom always knows what's good. Always.

When it comes to nuts, pecans are in a class of their own. With intense notes of floral aroma that turn so sweet, woodsy, and fragrant once toasted, they are perfect in desserts and savory applications alike, from slow cooker candied pecans to peach & burrata caprese. If you're looking to incorporate more fiber, protein, calcium, and iron into your diet, these butter pecan chicken bites are a great place to start.

A tip: Get a range of fineness in your chopped pecans. You can use your food processor and pulse until there's a good mixture of powder-like pecans and crushed pecans. Bigger pieces provide crunch and texture; finer pieces provide better coverage for an even coating on your chicken bites. Don't skip out on the dipping sauce if you're a fan of honey mustard—it's the perfect condiment for these snackable bites!

Chicken has a terrible reputation for drying out in the oven, but with chicken thighs you don't need to worry. These boneless chicken thighs first get marinated in an easy honey mustard-esque sauce that makes them so juicy and flavorful. Baking the thighs at a high temp means less time spent in the oven with even better results. These thighs are great served with a big salad or some roasted potatoes.

The longer you let your chicken marinade the better they will be. If you only have time for a 30 minute marinade, leave the chicken out at room temperature. If marinating for any longer than an hour go ahead and let it marinade in the fridge. You can let your chicken marinate overnight in the refrigerator if needed. I like to take my chicken out and leave at room temperature while the oven preheats. Going straight from the refrigerator to the oven can cause your chicken to tighten up and get tough.

beef

Combine Ground Beef and steak seasoning in large bowl, mixing lightly but thoroughly. Lightly shape into four 1/2-inch thick patties.
Place patties on grid over medium, ash-covered coals. Grill, covered, 8 to 10 minutes (over medium heat on preheated gas grill, 7 to 9 minutes) until instant-read thermometer inserted horizontally into center registers 160°F, turning occasionally. About 2 minutes before burgers are done, place buns, cut sides down, on grid. Grill until lightly toasted. During last minute of grilling, top each burger with cheese slice.

Cook's Tip:

To prepare on stovetop, heat large nonstick skillet over medium heat until hot. Place patties in skillet; cook 12 to 15 minutes until instant-read thermometer inserted horizontally into center registers 160°F, turning occasionally.

Cooking times are for fresh or thoroughly thawed ground beef. Ground beef should be cooked to an internal temperature of 160°F. Color is not a reliable indicator of ground beef doneness.
